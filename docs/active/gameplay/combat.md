# Combat & Operational Missions

Tick-based combat engine and non-trade mission system. Covers pirate bounty encounters, timed patrol/survey missions, and the battle resolution mechanics that faction wars will later extend.

---

## Operational Mission Types

Five mission types generated by the `missions` tick processor, independent of factions:

| Type | Stat Gate | Duration | Trigger | Reward Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Patrol** | Firepower ≥ 5 | 15–25 ticks | System danger > 0.15 | 200–500 CR (scales with danger) |
| **Survey** | Sensors ≥ 6 | 10–15 ticks | System has survey-eligible trait | 150–400 CR (scales with trait quality) |
| **Bounty** | Firepower ≥ 4, Hull ≥ 30 | Battle-driven | System danger > 0.20 | 300–800 CR (scales with enemy tier) |
| **Salvage** | Stealth ≥ 4 | 12–20 ticks | System has salvage-eligible trait | 200–600 CR |
| **Recon** | Stealth ≥ 5 | 8–12 ticks | System danger > 0.10 + recon-eligible trait (dual-gated) | 250–550 CR |

### Mission Lifecycle

Two-step accept/start flow:

1. **Generated** — candidates are created from system danger levels and traits.
2. **Available** — displayed on the system's Operations board (Contracts tab → Operations sub-tab).
3. **Accepted** — the player claims the mission. It's reserved for them, but no ship is committed yet. Max 5 active operational missions per player.
4. **Started** — the player assigns a ship and begins the mission. The ship must pass the stat gates, be docked at the mission's target system, and not be disabled, in a convoy, or committed to another mission.
   - Patrol/Survey/Salvage/Recon: the ship is committed for the mission's duration (it can't navigate or trade until done).
   - Bounty: the battle begins the moment the mission starts; the ship is locked until the battle resolves.
5. **Completed** — timed missions finish on schedule; bounties resolve through combat.
6. **Failed** — the deadline passes, the ship is destroyed/disabled, or the player abandons it.

### Generation Sources

- **Danger-based** (patrol, bounty): Computed from event modifiers + government danger baseline + trait danger (`computeTraitDanger`). Higher danger = more missions and tougher enemies.
- **Trait-based** (survey): Systems with survey-eligible traits (15 total): `precursor_ruins`, `gravitational_anomaly`, `binary_star`, `dark_nebula`, `subspace_rift`, `bioluminescent_ecosystem`, `exotic_matter_traces`, `captured_rogue_body`, `crystalline_formations`, `nebula_proximity`, `pulsar_proximity`, `ion_storm_corridor`, `tidally_locked_world`, `signal_anomaly`, `xenobiology_preserve`. Trait quality affects reward.
- **Trait-based** (salvage): Systems with salvage-eligible traits: `generation_ship_wreckage`, `orbital_ring_remnant`, `shipbreaking_yards`, `derelict_fleet`, `abandoned_station`.
- **Dual-gated** (recon): Systems with recon-eligible traits AND danger > 0.10: `dark_nebula`, `nebula_proximity`, `ion_storm_corridor`, `smuggler_haven`, `ancient_minefield`, `pirate_stronghold`.

### Constraints

- Max 4 available operational missions per system (`OP_MISSION_CAP_PER_SYSTEM`)
- Unclaimed missions expire after 200 ticks (`OP_MISSION_DEADLINE_TICKS`)
- One ship per mission (no convoy missions yet)
- Ship must be docked, not disabled, not already committed to another mission

## Combat Engine

The model that resolves a battle. It's deliberately self-contained — all combat state goes in and a result comes out — so the same rules can later drive fleet battles and faction wars.

### Combat Stats

**Player side** — derived from ship stats:

| Stat | Formula |
|---|---|
| Strength | `hullCurrent + shieldCurrent` |
| Damage per round | `firepower × 1.5` (FIREPOWER_TO_DAMAGE) |
| Damage reduction | `evasion / (evasion + 20)`, capped at 40% (diminishing returns) |
| Starting morale | `85 + (hullCurrent / hullMax × 15)` (healthy ships fight better) |

**Enemy side** — derived from tier + system danger:

| Tier | Danger Threshold | Base Strength | Base Damage/Round | Base Morale | Base Damage Reduction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weak (Pirate Scouts) | < 0.25 | 40 | 5 | 60 | 5% |
| Moderate (Pirate Raiders) | 0.25–0.39 | 70 | 10 | 70 | 12% |
| Strong (Pirate Warlord) | ≥ 0.40 | 110 | 16 | 80 | 20% |

All enemy stats (strength, damage) scale by `0.6 + dangerLevel × 0.8` — at danger 0 enemies are 60% strength, at danger 0.5 they reach full base values.

### Round Resolution

Rounds resolve every 6 ticks (`ROUND_INTERVAL`). Each round:

1. **Damage calculation** — both sides deal damage simultaneously
   - Raw damage = `damagePerRound × (1 + variance)` where variance is ±20%
   - Net damage = raw × `(1 - target.damageReduction)`
2. **Strength update** — subtract net damage from strength (min 0)
3. **Morale shift** — the side that took more damage loses morale proportional to the casualty ratio
   - Base loss: 3 morale for the losing side, base gain: 2 for the winning side
   - Lopsided rounds (>2:1 damage ratio): additional 8 morale swing
4. **Battle end check** — after each round:
   - Strength ≤ 0 → destroyed (victory or defeat)
   - Morale ≤ 15 → retreat (player_retreat or enemy_retreat)

### Battle Outcomes

| Outcome | Ship Damage | Reward | Mission Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| Player Victory | Proportional to strength lost (hull/shield) | Full reward | Completed |
| Enemy Retreat | Same as victory | 60% reward | Completed |
| Player Defeat | Heavy damage, possible disable | None | Failed |
| Player Retreat | Moderate damage | None | Failed |

**Damage translation**: Battle strength loss maps back to ship hull/shield damage proportionally. If a ship entered with 80 strength (50 hull + 30 shield) and exits with 40, it lost 50% — shield absorbs first, then hull.

## The Battle Viewer

When a ship is in combat, its detail page shows a live battle display so the player can follow the fight as it unfolds round by round:

- **Strength bars** for both sides (player and enemy), shown as a percentage of remaining strength.
- **Morale labels** that translate the morale number into a readable state: Confident (high), Steady, Shaken, then Breaking (about to retreat).
- **Round history** — a turn-by-turn log of damage dealt and taken, so the outcome never feels arbitrary.
- **Status** — whether the battle is in progress or has ended (Victory, Defeated, Retreated, or Enemy Retreated).

Elsewhere in the UI, ships committed to an active battle are flagged with an "In Battle" badge, and operational missions sit alongside trade missions in the player's active-missions view.

## Extensibility

The combat engine is designed for reuse by the faction war system (Layer 2):

- **Fleet battles**: `derivePlayerCombatStats` can aggregate across multiple ships. The round resolution math (simultaneous damage, morale cascades) scales to fleet-level engagements.
- **Enemy variety**: The enemy tier system can be extended beyond pirates — faction navies, mercenary bands, etc. Just add new tier definitions.
- **Stratagems**: Round resolution can accept modifier functions for special abilities, tactical choices, or terrain effects.
- **Reinforcements**: The battle model supports adding/removing combatants between rounds.
- **Siege mechanics**: Strength/morale framework maps naturally to fortification assault with defender advantages.
